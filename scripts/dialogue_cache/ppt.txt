A company might have excellent ESG scores internally, but still contribute to a Hollow Win if its core business model relies on degrading a vital system. PST offers a more rigorous, mathematically grounded framework for understanding the *mechanisms* by which private actions interact with system welfare, going beyond qualitative assessments to quantitative projections of T* and welfare destruction. Doughnut Economics, on the other hand, provides a powerful *target* – a safe and just operating space for humanity. Our framework provides the *toolset* to measure our progress (or regress) towards or away from that doughnut, specifically by identifying and accounting for the private-systemic tensions that push us out of it. So, they are complementary. Our work provides the granular, actionable measurement and governance framework to help achieve the ambitions of models like Doughnut Economics and elevate ESG to a systemic level.
